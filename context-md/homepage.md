## Goal of the page
- Introduce the brand and style of the ceramic shop
- Show a small, curated set of hero products and collections
- Build trust with story + craftsmanship details
- Make it very easy to start shopping or contact the studio

## Target audience
- People who appreciate handmade / small-batch ceramics
- Interior design‑curious shoppers looking for unique pieces
- Gift buyers (housewarming, weddings, holidays)

## Page structure

### 1. Hero section
- Large, clean hero image of signature ceramic pieces on a neutral background
- Short headline (1–2 lines) that describes the brand (e.g. “Handcrafted ceramics for calm, modern homes”)
- Supporting sentence emphasizing small-batch, handmade, and location
- Primary CTA: “Shop collection”
- Secondary CTA: “Visit the studio” or “Learn our story”

### 2. Featured collection / Bestsellers
- 3–6 highlighted products or sets (e.g. mugs, bowls, vases)
- Each item: photo, name, short descriptor (e.g. “Matte stoneware mug”), price
- Quick “Add to cart” or “View details” action
- Optional text link: “View all products”

### 3. Shop by category
- Grid of 3–4 categories (e.g. Mugs, Bowls, Vases, Tableware)
- Each category tile: lifestyle image + short label
- Clicking a tile goes to a filtered shop view for that category

### 4. Brand story / About the studio
- Photo of the maker or studio space
- Short paragraph about the philosophy (materials, process, inspiration)
- Bullet list of 3 key points (e.g. “Small-batch”, “Dishwasher safe”, “Made in [City]”)
- CTA link: “Read the full story” (to About page)

### 5. Craftsmanship & process
- Visual strip or two-column layout showing:
  - Clay selection / preparation
  - Throwing / hand‑building
  - Glazing + firing
- Short copy highlighting durability, food safety, and uniqueness of each piece

### 6. Social proof (Reviews / Instagram)
- 2–3 customer quotes with star ratings
- Optional Instagram-style image grid of real homes using the pieces
- CTA: “See more reviews” or “Follow on Instagram”

### 7. Newsletter / Studio updates
- Simple card inviting users to join the mailing list
- One-sentence value prop (e.g. “Be first to know about new drops and restocks.”)
- Email input + “Join the list” button

### 8. FAQ preview
- 3–4 common questions:
  - Shipping and delivery times
  - Breakage / returns
  - Care instructions (dishwasher, microwave, oven)
  - Custom / commissioned pieces
- Links to full FAQ page

### 9. Final CTA strip
- Narrow section near footer with a simple, strong message:
  - Headline: “Bring warmth to your daily rituals”
  - Buttons: “Shop all ceramics” and “Explore gifts”

## Tone & visual style notes
- Minimal, calm, and warm (neutrals + one accent color)
- Lots of whitespace around products and text
- Focus on large, high-quality imagery of ceramics in real-life contexts
- Copy should be clear and inviting, not overly “marketing‑speak”
